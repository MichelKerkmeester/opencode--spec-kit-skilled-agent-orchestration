# 009-prototype-backlog: Record Flagged Prototype Candidates

## 1. Scope
This sub-phase records prototype-only candidates that survived the research but were not approved for immediate implementation. It covers weak-result lexical fallback, connected-doc hints, bounded wake-up/read formatting, quote-carrying evidence snippets, and temporal-fact follow-up discussion. It does not promote any of them to default runtime behavior.

## 3. Architecture Constraints
Every backlog candidate must remain non-authoritative, flag-only, and subordinate to the preserved retrieval, bootstrap, save, and graph surfaces. Prototype work cannot silently change ranking, scope, or governance defaults.

## 4. Success Criteria
- The phase records which candidates are prototype-only and why.
- The phase separates connected-doc hints and lexical fallback from approved build-now work.
- The phase keeps temporal-fact and evidence-snippet ideas explicitly deferred behind authority review.

## 5. Out of Scope
- Promoting any prototype to default behavior.
- Creating a second retrieval core, bootstrap authority, or storage substrate.
- Reopening rejected backend or taxonomy proposals.
