### Survival verdict
**weakened**

Justification (≤200 words).
The combo still has real leverage: a cached startup payload plus a retrieval nudge is better than cold replay plus raw grep. None of those kill the pattern outright, but together they downgrade the claim from "strong default combo" to "conditional win when summary fidelity and graph readiness are both high." The combo survives as a net-positive adapter, not as a generally safe multiplier.

## Combo 3 — Expose trustworthy structural context as reusable projections

### Synergy hypothesis (restated)
> CodeSight's detector discipline makes provenance explicit at extraction time, Graphify's evidence tiers carry that honesty into consumer-facing responses, and the static-artifact-plus-overlay pattern packages those trustworthy structures into durable briefs with live precision follow-up. The combined result is a reusable structural context layer that is both portable and inspectable. [SOURCE: research/iterations/q-f-killer-combos.md:55-55]

### 5 failure modes
1. **Confidence semantics collide**: in CodeSight, `confidence` means detection method (`ast` vs `regex`); in Graphify, `confidence` means evidence status (`EXTRACTED` vs `INFERRED` vs `AMBIGUOUS`). Treating them as one trust vocabulary misleads consumers at the schema boundary. [SOURCE: 002-codesight/external/src/types.ts:54-65] [SOURCE: 004-graphify/external/graphify/validate.py:4-7]

### Survival verdict

Justification (≤200 words).
The synergy hypothesis depends on one thing above all: confidence labels must make the structural layer more trustworthy than the pieces alone. The stress-test found the opposite.
