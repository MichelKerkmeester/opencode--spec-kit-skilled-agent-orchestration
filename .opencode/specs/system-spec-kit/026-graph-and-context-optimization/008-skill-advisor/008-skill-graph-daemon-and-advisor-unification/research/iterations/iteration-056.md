## Analysis
E and F compose around the same control-plane idea: **one workspace writer, many readers**. Track E concluded that cross-runtime safety requires a single-writer lease because the second concurrent writer is already enough to move WAL contention onto the hot path. Track F, in turn, deliberately kept migration side effects out of prompt-time reads: F3 makes backfill daemon-owned, and F4 makes rollback a controlled metadata demotion plus reindex.
