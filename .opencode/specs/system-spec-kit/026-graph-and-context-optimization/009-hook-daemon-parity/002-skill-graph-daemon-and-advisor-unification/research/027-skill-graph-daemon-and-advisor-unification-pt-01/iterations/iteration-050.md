## Analysis
The current scorer already tells us the lane ordering that Phase 027 should preserve after normalization. Direct author-controlled evidence is strongest today: explicit skill-name mention gets a larger boost than any lexical or graph heuristic, while ordinary keyword/name/corpus matches form the main deterministic body of the scorer. That aligns with r01's architecture sketch, which treats `explicit_author` and `lexical/phrase` as the primary prompt-time evidence surfaces and reserves graph/causal traversal for bounded assistance rather than first-pass authority. `.opencode/skill/skill-advisor/scripts/skill_advisor.py:2421-2449,2467-2477`, `.opencode/skill/skill-advisor/scripts/skill_advisor.py:552-620`, `.opencode/specs/system-spec-kit/026-graph-and-context-optimization/009-hook-daemon-parity/002-skill-graph-daemon-and-advisor-unification/research/027-skill-graph-daemon-and-advisor-unification-pt-01/research.md:100-106`

The numerical problem is not that the repo lacks weights; it is that the current additive constants are unnormalized and still allow the wrong lanes to inherit threshold-reaching power. `intent_signals` and `derived.trigger_phrases` are merged into one signal map, and that shared lane can contribute up to `3.0`, which is larger than the current lexical boosts and enough to trigger the intent-boost confidence curve by itself. r01 explicitly says generated phrases must stop masquerading as direct intent and that derived-only evidence cannot clear the default threshold unless a later precision gate authorizes it. That means the initial normalized fusion should intentionally separate and downweight `derived_generated`, not merely copy today's signal constants into TypeScript. `.opencode/skill/skill-advisor/scripts/skill_advisor.py:180-219,623-660`, `.opencode/specs/system-spec-kit/026-graph-and-context-optimization/009-hook-daemon-parity/002-skill-graph-daemon-and-advisor-unification/research/027-skill-graph-daemon-and-advisor-unification-pt-01/research.md:116-116,135-137,152-159`

The same logic makes `semantic-shadow` easy to calibrate at launch: its initial analytical weight should be **0.00 in the live routing score**, even if the implementation records semantic attribution in shadow mode. The current semantic multiplier can already produce threshold-reaching boosts on its own, but C6/R5/G5/G6 all say prompt-time semantic and learned channels remain shadow-only until they clear latency, parity, and holdout gates. So the safest adopt-now baseline is a normalized live vector of **`explicit_author = 0.45`, `lexical = 0.30`, `graph/causal = 0.15`, `derived_generated = 0.10`, `semantic-shadow = 0.00`**, with ablation/tuning bands of **В±0.05**, **В±0.05**, **В±0.03**, **В±0.02**, and **0.00 until promotion** respectively. This preserves the current dominance ordering, caps derived below any threshold-clearing role, keeps graph as a tiebreak/disambiguation helper, and leaves semantic influence fully behind the later promotion gates. `.opencode/skill/skill-advisor/scripts/skill_advisor.py:1484-1489,1789-1815,2013-2058,2111-2165`, `.opencode/specs/system-spec-kit/026-graph-and-context-optimization/009-hook-daemon-parity/002-skill-graph-daemon-and-advisor-unification/research/027-skill-graph-daemon-and-advisor-unification-pt-01/next-research-paths.md:72-76,122-127`, `.opencode/skill/skill-advisor/feature_catalog/feature_catalog.md:439-447,631-635`
