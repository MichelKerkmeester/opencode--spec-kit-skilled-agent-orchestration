## Analysis
At the architecture level, F and G are meant to compose in sequence, not compete. The roadmap already makes lifecycle/trust-lane work an upstream prerequisite for the native advisor core, which means F is supposed to define what counts as active, superseded, rollback-demoted, or derived-eligible **before** G calibrates weights, parity, and promotion behavior. The adjustment is that the current runtime shape is still too pre-F to make G's calibration/parity package sufficient on its own. Live loaders currently collect authored `intent_signals` and `derived.trigger_phrases` into one signal map whenever `derived` exists, and the signal lane can contribute enough boost to satisfy today's confidence curve. That is exactly the pre-X3 / pre-F trust problem: F1/F2/F5 want lifecycle-aware haircuts and exclusions for aging, superseded, archived, and future skills, while F3/F4 require mixed-version and rollback states where v1 skills remain routable but must not look identical to fully derived v2 skills.
