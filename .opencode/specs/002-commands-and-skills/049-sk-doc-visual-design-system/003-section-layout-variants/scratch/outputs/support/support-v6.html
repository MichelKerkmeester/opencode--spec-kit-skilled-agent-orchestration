Resolution Ladder

Start at the lowest rung and escalate only when exit criteria are not met. The ladder format makes ownership, response timing, and escalation boundaries explicit.

Rung 1 Low impact

Self-serve correction

TriggerSingle command fails but rerun is possible.
First responseRun setup verification and apply suggested fix.
Escalate whenIssue persists after 2 reproducible attempts.
Rung 2 Medium impact

Team-assisted triage

TriggerValidation warnings block delivery or cause drift.
First responseAttach logs, exact command, and environment mode.
Escalate whenNo mitigation within agreed triage window.
Rung 3 High impact

On-call intervention

TriggerService outage, data risk, or repeated production failure.
First responseOpen incident channel and freeze non-critical deploys.
Escalate whenRecovery target is missed or blast radius expands.
Rung 4 Critical

Leadership and post-incident review

TriggerCross-team impact with unresolved customer-facing issue.
First responseActivate incident commander and shared status updates.
Exit criteriaRoot cause documented and rollback or fix verified.

Common Questions

What evidence is required before escalation?

Capture exact command, output excerpt, runtime mode, and timestamp. Escalations without evidence delay triage.

How do we avoid over-escalating?

Stay on the current rung until the exit criteria fails. Escalation is based on impact and elapsed response window, not urgency alone.

When should customer communication start?

At rung 3 or sooner if the issue affects external users, data integrity, or contract-bound uptime.

If any step indicates possible data loss, skip directly to rung 3 and open an incident bridge immediately.
