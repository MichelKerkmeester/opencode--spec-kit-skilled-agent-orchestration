## Findings

1. **High:** `session-types.ts` is documented as the canonical collected-session contract, but the loader path still uses a parallel type family from `input-normalizer.ts` and bridges the gap with casts. `session-types.ts` says it “eliminates parallel type hierarchies” and is the single source of truth ([session-types.ts](/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Opencode%20Env/Public/.opencode/skill/system-spec-kit/scripts/types/session-types.ts#L8), [README.md](/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Opencode%20Env/Public/.opencode/skill/system-spec-kit/scripts/types/README.md#L151)), but `data-loader.ts` imports `Observation`, `UserPrompt`, `RecentContext`, and `FileEntry` from [input-normalizer.ts](/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Opencode%20Env/Public/.opencode/skill/system-spec-kit/scripts/utils/input-normalizer.ts#L22) instead of [session-types.ts](/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Opencode%20Env/Public/.opencode/skill/system-spec-kit/scripts/types/session-types.ts#L55), then returns repeated `as LoadedData` casts in the native/file branches ([data-loader.ts](/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Opencode%20Env/Public/.opencode/skill/system-spec-kit/scripts/loaders/data-loader.ts#L20), [data-loader.ts](/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Opencode%20Env/Public/.opencode/skill/system-spec-kit/scripts/loaders/data-loader.ts#L346), [data-loader.ts](/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Opencode%20Env/Public/.opencode/skill/system-spec-kit/scripts/loaders/data-loader.ts#L527)). The mismatch is real at the workflow boundary: `runWorkflow()` expects `loadDataFn?: () => Promise<CollectedDataFull>` ([workflow.ts](/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Opencode%20Env/Public/.opencode/skill/system-spec-kit/scripts/core/workflow.ts#L93)), while `generate-context.ts` passes `loadCollectedData()` ([generate-context.ts](/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Opencode%20Env/Public/.opencode/skill/system-spec-kit/scripts/memory/generate-context.ts#L588)). That makes schema drift between loader-normalizer data and extractor expectations largely compile-time invisible.

2. **Medium:** tool-layer and handler-layer request contracts are duplicated instead of shared, so compatibility currently depends on structural typing rather than a single exported interface. `ContextArgs`, `SearchArgs`, and `SaveArgs` are defined in [tools/types.ts](/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Opencode%20Env/Public/.opencode/skill/system-spec-kit/mcp_server/tools/types.ts#L41), but `memory-context.ts`, `memory-search.ts`, and `handlers/save/types.ts` each define their own handler-side versions ([memory-context.ts](/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Opencode%20Env/Public/.opencode/skill/system-spec-kit/mcp_server/handlers/memory-context.ts#L96), [memory-search.ts](/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Opencode%20Env/Public/.opencode/skill/system-spec-kit/mcp_server/handlers/memory-search.ts#L126), [save/types.ts](/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Opencode%20Env/Public/.opencode/skill/system-spec-kit/mcp_server/handlers/save/types.ts#L106)). The dispatch layer passes the tool version into handlers ([context-tools.ts](/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Opencode%20Env/Public/.opencode/skill/system-spec-kit/mcp_server/tools/context-tools.ts#L13), [memory-tools.ts](/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Opencode%20Env/Public/.opencode/skill/system-spec-kit/mcp_server/tools/memory-tools.ts#L43)), but the handler signatures use different local types ([memory-context.ts](/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Opencode%20Env/Public/.opencode/skill/system-spec-kit/mcp_server/handlers/memory-context.ts#L547), [memory-search.ts](/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Opencode%20Env/Public/.opencode/skill/system-spec-kit/mcp_server/handlers/memory-search.ts#L728), [memory-save.ts](/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Opencode%20Env/Public/.opencode/skill/system-spec-kit/mcp_server/handlers/memory-save.ts#L706)). `SearchArgs` has already drifted: tool-side `mode` and `minState` are constrained unions, while handler-side they are plain `string` ([tools/types.ts](/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Opencode%20Env/Public/.opencode/skill/system-spec-kit/mcp_server/tools/types.ts#L84), [memory-search.ts](/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Opencode%20Env/Public/.opencode/skill/system-spec-kit/mcp_server/handlers/memory-search.ts#L147)).

3. **Medium:** save/index response contracts are fragmented across the save stack even though `handlers/save/types.ts` already exists as a natural canonical home. `IndexResult` is defined separately in [handlers/save/types.ts](/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Opencode%20Env/Public/.opencode/skill/system-spec-kit/mcp_server/handlers/save/types.ts#L37), [pe-gating.ts](/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Opencode%20Env/Public/.opencode/skill/system-spec-kit/mcp_server/handlers/pe-gating.ts#L51), [chunking-orchestrator.ts](/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Opencode%20Env/Public/.opencode/skill/system-spec-kit/mcp_server/handlers/chunking-orchestrator.ts#L47), [memory-index.ts](/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Opencode%20Env/Public/.opencode/skill/system-spec-kit/mcp_server/handlers/memory-index.ts#L54), and [context-server.ts](/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Opencode%20Env/Public/.opencode/skill/system-spec-kit/mcp_server/context-server.ts#L106). `AtomicSaveResult` is also split between [handlers/save/types.ts](/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Opencode%20Env/Public/.opencode/skill/system-spec-kit/mcp_server/handlers/save/types.ts#L90) and [transaction-manager.ts](/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Opencode%20Env/Public/.opencode/skill/system-spec-kit/mcp_server/lib/storage/transaction-manager.ts#L31). These may be private today, but they are the same concepts with different field sets, which is exactly the kind of drift that later forces more `Record<string, unknown>` patching.

4. **Medium:** the biggest type-safety escape hatch in the MCP boundary is still `parseArgs<T>()`, which double-casts arbitrary records into target types and even returns `{}` as `T` for non-objects. See [tools/types.ts](/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Opencode%20Env/Public/.opencode/skill/system-spec-kit/mcp_server/tools/types.ts#L19). There is no `any` in the audited files, but there is heavy reliance on `unknown`, `Record<string, unknown>`, and index signatures in the exact places where contracts matter most. `GraphSearchFn` is another weak seam: it erases both options and results to generic records ([search-types.ts](/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Opencode%20Env/Public/.opencode/skill/system-spec-kit/mcp_server/lib/search/search-types.ts#L7)), and `createUnifiedGraphSearchFn()` implements that loose shape directly ([graph-search-fn.ts](/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Opencode%20Env/Public/.opencode/skill/system-spec-kit/mcp_server/lib/search/graph-search-fn.ts#L453)). That prevents the graph channel from participating in the stronger pipeline typing used elsewhere.

5. **Low:** the loader and renderer modules themselves are active, not dead. `loadCollectedData()` is used by the CLI entrypoint ([generate-context.ts](/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Opencode%20Env/Public/.opencode/skill/system-spec-kit/scripts/memory/generate-context.ts#L26)), and `populateTemplate()` is used by the main workflow ([workflow.ts](/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Opencode%20Env/Public/.opencode/skill/system-spec-kit/scripts/core/workflow.ts#L1903)). The barrels are also live because those imports come through [loaders/index.ts](/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Opencode%20Env/Public/.opencode/skill/system-spec-kit/scripts/loaders/index.ts#L9) and [renderers/index.ts](/Users/michelkerkmeester/MEGA/Development/Opencode%20Env/Public/.opencode/skill/system-spec-kit/scripts/renderers/index.ts#L9). What does look dead or test-only is some exported surface around them: repo-wide search only found `LoadedData`, `TemplateContext`, `MCPResponseWithContext`, and `parseValidatedArgs()` at their definition/re-export sites, and `renderTemplate()` appears to be exercised only by tests, not production call paths.

## Notes

- Handler/tool contracts are currently **compatible by structure**, not by shared source. That means they work now, but drift will be caught late.
- The strongest “missing export” candidates are `ContextArgs`, `SearchArgs`, `SaveArgs`, `IndexResult`, and `AtomicSaveResult`: all exist in more than one place and should probably have one canonical owner.
- I did not find `any` in the audited files; the problem is mostly `unknown` plus broad records and casts, not explicit `any`.
